A sign language recognizer is a technology or system designed to interpret and understand sign language gestures made by individuals, typically using computer vision or sensor-based technologies. Sign language recognition systems can vary widely in complexity and functionality, but they generally involve capturing and analyzing video or sensor data of sign language gestures and translating them into text or spoken language, or directly into commands for computer applications.

Some sign language recognizers use machine learning algorithms to interpret and classify gestures, while others may rely on predefined gesture dictionaries or rule-based approaches. These systems can be used for various purposes, including communication aids for individuals with hearing impairments, interfaces for controlling electronic devices or software applications through gestures, and tools for automatic translation between sign language and spoken or written language.

Overall, sign language recognizers play an important role in bridging communication gaps between people who use sign language and those who do not, enabling more inclusive interactions in various contexts.
